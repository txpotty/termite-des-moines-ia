Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Identifying and Addressing Termite Infestation Issues

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early is crucial for minimizing damage and the associated repair costs. Unfortunately, termites are often undetected until significant damage has occurred. At Des Moines Termite, we are experts at identifying even subtle signs of termite activity and implementing effective solutions. Here are some common problems homeowners in Ankeny might encounter and how our services address them:

Visible Mud Tubes

What it means: Mud tubes are typically constructed by subterranean termites to provide a protected pathway from their underground colony to a food source (your home's wooden structure). They are often found on foundation walls, in crawl spaces, or along floor joists.
How we solve it: The presence of mud tubes is a strong indicator of active subterranean termites. Our technicians will trace these tubes back to the source of activity and apply targeted treatments to the soil and affected areas. This often involves creating a liquid termiticide barrier around the foundation to eliminate existing termites and prevent future entry. We also may use baiting systems in conjunction with or as an alternative to liquid treatments, which are designed to be shared amongst the colony, leading to its eventual collapse. The chosen method depends on the severity of the infestation and property specifics.

Wood Damage (Hollow Sounding Wood)

What it means: Termites consume wood from the inside out, leaving a thin outer layer. When you tap on wood that has been damaged by termites, it may sound hollow.
How we solve it: Detecting hollow-sounding wood during an inspection points to potential internal termite activity. We will further investigate the extent of the damage and identify the active infestation areas. Treatment for wood damage involves targeting the termites directly within the affected structures. This could include localized treatments using injectable termiticides or dusts that penetrate cracked or damaged wood. Our goal is to eliminate the termites actively feeding on your home's structure and prevent further destruction. While we eliminate the termites, addressing the structural damage itself may require a qualified contractor for repairs.

Discarded Termite Wings

What it means: Termite swarmers (winged termites) emerge from mature colonies to reproduce and establish new colonies. After swarming, they shed their wings, often leaving small piles of discarded wings near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures. Seeing these wings is a clear sign of a nearby termite colony.
How we solve it: The presence of discarded wings indicates a mature termite colony is present on or near your property. This signifies that the colony is old enough to reproduce and has reached a significant size. Our focus will be on locating and eliminating the source colony. This often involves a comprehensive treatment strategy addressing the soil around your foundation and potentially baiting systems to target the colony directly. Preventing future swarming events from your property is also a key objective of our treatment plan.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

What it means: Termite activity, particularly subterranean termites, can introduce moisture into walls and wooden structures, leading to bubbling or peeling paint.
How we solve it: Bubbling or peeling paint can be a subtle but important sign of hidden termite activity. Our technicians will investigate the area behind the affected paint to determine if termites are present. If termites are found, we will implement targeted treatments to eliminate them from the wall voids and surrounding structures. Addressing the underlying moisture issue that attracted the termites is also an important part of our solution, and we may provide recommendations for moisture control measures.

Small Piles of Fecal Pellets (Frass)

What it means: Drywood termites, which live entirely within the wood they infest, expel small, hard fecal pellets called frass. These pellets are often found in small piles near infested wood.
How we solve it: The presence of frass indicates a drywood termite infestation. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites do not require contact with the soil. Treatment for drywood termites often involves localized treatments, such as injecting termiticides into the galleries (tunnels) the termites have created within the wood. In cases of widespread drywood termite infestation, fumigation of the entire structure may be recommended as the most effective solution to ensure complete eradication. We will assess the severity of the infestation and recommend the most appropriate treatment method for your Ankeny home.
