Specific Ant Species and Their Challenges

Des Moines, IA is home to several common ant species, each with its own characteristics and challenges. Understanding the specific type of ant you're dealing with is crucial for effective treatment. While many DIY ant control products might offer temporary relief for some species, others require specialized approaches and professional expertise for complete elimination. Let's explore some of the common ant species found in our area and the specific challenges they present.

  • Pavement Ants: These are small, dark brown or black ants commonly found nesting under pavements, driveways, and concrete slabs. They often enter homes through cracks in foundations or walls. Pavement ants can be persistent and difficult to eliminate entirely without targeting their outdoor nests. Targeting the outdoor colony is key to controlling indoor pavement (515) 206-6667 ant problems. They will forage for food indoors, often in kitchens and pantries, but their primary nesting site is usually outdoors. Identifying and treating the nest is essential for long-term control.
  • Odorous House Ants: These small, dark brown ants emit a rotting coconut-like odor when crushed. They are known for forming large colonies and establishing multiple satellite nests. Odorous house ants are attracted to sweets and can quickly infest food preparation areas. Their ability to establish multiple nests makes them particularly challenging to eradicate without professional knowledge and techniques. These ants are notorious for being difficult to control with DIY methods. They can quickly move their satellite nests if disturbed. Targeting both the main colony and satellite nests is crucial for effective control. Our team utilizes professional-grade baits and treatments specifically designed for odorous house ants.
  • Carpenter Ants: While primarily a structural pest often treated separately, it's important to mention carpenter ants as they are a common concern. These large, black or reddish-black ants excavate tunnels in wood to create nests, potentially causing structural damage over time. Seeing large ants or small piles of sawdust can be a sign of carpenter ant activity, and immediate professional help is recommended to prevent further damage. Our team can help distinguish between different ant species and recommend the appropriate course of action, including carpenter ant treatments if necessary. Carpenter ants pose a significant threat to the structural integrity of your home if left untreated. They don't eat wood like termites; they tunnel through it. Identifying the location of the colony within the wood is critical. Our inspection process can pinpoint the areas of carpenter ant activity.
  • Acrobat Ants: These ants are named for their ability to raise their abdomen above the rest of their body when disturbed. They often nest in decaying wood, insulation, or under objects outdoors. Acrobat ants can also infest homes, often entering through openings around windows and doors. Targeting their nesting sites and sealing entry points are important for controlling these ants. These ants are often found nesting in damaged or decaying wood, making moisture control an important factor in preventing infestations.
